03 / FLAGSHIP KIT

The Meerkat: our whole toolkit on a trailer

The Meerkat is the unit most crews meet first. It's a road-legal trailer that carries the entire connectivity stack — a bonded-cellular router pulling from all four South African networks at once, a low-earth-orbit satellite terminal for when there's no cellular at all, and a directional microwave dish that can shoot a link up to twelve kilometres to a ridgeline with a clear view of town. Power comes from a 3.6 kWp solar array backed by a quiet inverter-generator, so it runs through a night shoot without anyone refuelling at the wrong moment. One field engineer tows it in, levels it, and has a network live in under ninety minutes — usually while the grips are still unloading. We built the first Meerkat in a Salt River workshop in 2017, after a commercial in the Cederberg lost a full day's dailies to a dead 3G tower. There are now four in the fleet, and a fifth being fitted out.

04 / THE GUARANTEE

First Light: dailies to post within two hours of wrap

Most of what we do is invisible when it works, so here's the one promise we put in writing. From the moment a shoot wraps for the day, we get the DIT's dailies to your post house within two hours — graded proxies, sound, and camera reports, checksummed and confirmed received. We call it First Light, because the point is that the editor in Cape Town or London opens the footage before the crew has finished breakfast the next morning. It works because we don't lean on a single pipe. The Meerkat bonds cellular and satellite, prioritises the transfer traffic, and fails over to the microwave link automatically if a channel drops. Every handover is logged, so if something does go wrong you hear about it before the wrap party, not three days into the edit. We've held the two-hour mark on 94% of shoot days across four years — the misses were weather, and we flag those in advance.

09 / FAQ

Questions from the call sheet

How fast can you be on location?

A rig can be on the road within 24 hours for Cape locations. Setup takes under 90 minutes from the moment we arrive.

What if there's genuinely no cellular and no clear sky?

We establish a microwave backhaul link to a ridgeline with line-of-sight to town coverage. We plan this entirely during the technical recce.

How big a file can First Light actually move?

It comfortably handles a typical proxy-and-sound daily package. We size the link to the camera package at the recce and tell you immediately if a specific codec won't make the transfer window.

Do you supply the on-set Wi-Fi too, or just the uplink?

Both. We broadcast a robust private network across the site, ensuring video village, the DIT cart, and the production office all stay online.

What does a shoot day cost?

We charge a fixed day rate for the rig and the field engineer, plus necessary travel. Multi-day and full-schedule rates are available and quoted per recce.

What happens to our footage on your kit?

Footage is heavily encrypted in transit. We physically wipe all files from our local kit within 24 hours of receiving confirmed post handover.

Commissions

Speak to the loft about your rig.

Timeline

From first call to first hoist.

Commissioning a heavy flax sail requires a rigorous exchange of information. The process begins with an initial call to discuss the vessel's history and the desired sail plan. Following this, we ask the owner to send chalk rubbings of the spar hardware by post. Once these dimensions are confirmed, a 30% deposit secures a slot on the cutting floor.

Our standard build time is fourteen weeks, during which the canvas is cut, laid, and seamed by hand. For owners within a day's drive of Argyll, we conduct an on-site fitting prior to hand-roping the luff. The final invoice is issued only when the sail sets perfectly upon its spars.

FAQ

Commissioning questions.

How long does a flax sail last?

Properly handled, a heavy 16-oz flax mainsail stitched with waxed twine can easily last forty years. The crucial factor is keeping the cloth ventilated when furled and returning it to a loft for reproofing and a seam inspection every six to eight years.

Can you match a sail to an archival photograph?

Yes. Many of our Museum Commissions involve working from a single grainy photograph taken in 1912. We can identify the likely cloth weight, the panel widths, and the specific cut of the roach to recreate the profile identically.

Do you re-proof and repair sails you did not cut?

We will repair and reproof traditional canvas sails cut by other lofts, provided they are made of natural fibres. We categorically refuse to repair, alter, or work upon any sail made of Dacron, Kevlar, or bonded polyester.

What if my boat is not in the UK?

We regularly undertake international commissions. The process remains the same, relying on posted chalk rubbings of your gaff jaws and thorough video calls to verify the geometry of the rig before the first panel is cut on the floor.

Are your sails insurable for racing?

Our traditional methods meet or exceed the structural requirements laid out by major maritime underwriters for classic yacht racing. The solid bronze cringles and heavy hand-roping provide immense strength at critical load points.

Do you offer finance on Full Wardrobe commissions?

We do not operate a finance scheme. Our standard structure applies universally: a 30% deposit to secure your place on the cutting floor, with the remaining balance due upon successful completion and final fitting of the wardrobe.

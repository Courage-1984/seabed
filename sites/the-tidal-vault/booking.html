The Marine FAQ

Do you accept corporate occupational health insurance?

Yes. The Tidal Vault is registered as an approved restorative facility with major maritime insurers including Bupa Corporate, Allianz Maritime, and standard P&I Clubs. Intake coordinators will process your industrial medical clearance directly with your fleet HR division.

What should I wear during the therapy?

All necessary garments are provided. For the hyper-saline flotation and bladderwrack lamination, we supply heavy organic cotton robes and marine-grade protective footwear. Within the chambers themselves, complete exposure is recommended to maximize skin contact with the marine minerals, though clinical modesty garments are available.

How does the high salinity affect minor cuts from hand tool work?

Our North Sea water is saturated with Epsom salts to a density of 1.28. While highly sterile, this extreme salinity will sharply sting open abrasions. We provide a medical-grade barrier petroleum jelly to seal any minor cuts or tool scrapes prior to entering the vault.

Do you treat deep-sea decompression illnesses?

No, we are strictly a non-hyperbaric facility. The Tidal Vault focuses exclusively on vestibular exhaustion and acoustic trauma. Any suspected decompression sickness must be routed immediately to a hyperbaric medical center.

What are the cancellation policies if a ship's rotation changes due to weather?

We understand that offshore schedules are dictated by the sea state. We offer a zero-penalty, rolling hold policy for all maritime professionals. If your SOV is delayed by heavy weather, your residency is automatically preserved until your crew transfer vessel makes port.

Travel & Shore Logistics

Reaching our subterranean sanctuary is designed to minimize any further travel fatigue. The Tidal Vault operates a private, low-vibration shuttle service that collects engineers directly from the docks at Ramsgate Port and the Port of Dover. For teams arriving by air, direct overland transfers are available from the London Heliport and regional commercial terminals. By road, the Broadstairs Tidal Bathing Pool is accessible via the M2 and A299, dropping directly onto the Isle of Thanet coastal route. Rail connections are equally robust, with the High Speed 1 (HS1) line connecting London St Pancras to Broadstairs station in under eighty minutes, where our intake coordinators will meet you directly at the platform to secure your gear.

The Active Registry

Tracking the Global Fleet

We maintain a rigorous, physical logbook detailing every piece of heavy gear that passes through our Scapa Flow doors. Upon completion, each helmet is issued a stamped, traceable serial number that securely ties its new structural records to the original manufacturer's stamps. This process creates an unbroken chain of accountability. Our active registry currently tracks operational restorations deployed from the frigid oil fields off Bergen all the way down to commercial salvage operations near the Falklands. This meticulous cataloguing ensures that the structural history and certification status of any Oxide & Tide refit can be instantly verified, providing absolute transparency for offshore masters, collectors, and marine archaeology institutions worldwide.

Tier Specifications

Commercial Investment Tiers

We believe in transparent pricing that accurately reflects the demanding hourly requirements of complex metalwork. Our services are categorized into three precise tiers. Conservation-grade stabilization, focused primarily on halting galvanic corrosion and removing surface contaminants for secure storage, begins at £1,800. Structural Restoration, which involves localized patching, spitcock repairs, and replacing standard seals for dry exhibition, typically requires an investment of £3,500. Material Logistics

Navigating Maritime Heritage Laws

Moving historical brass and copper apparatus across international maritime boundaries is a highly regulated process. Accurate manifest declarations are crucial to prevent equipment seizures at customs checkpoints. When shipping a helmet to our workshop, clients must complete specific UK Maritime Heritage declarations, clearly defining the piece as an antique undergoing repair rather than a commercial import. This careful documentation usually qualifies the freight for temporary import tax exemptions. Furthermore, your bill of lading must precisely detail the helmet's weight, dimensions, and declared historical value. We routinely assist our international clients in navigating these complex legal logistics to ensure safe, unobstructed transit to Orkney.

Commission FAQ

Preservation and Warranty

Do you replace original viewport glass with modern materials?

For Class A operational refits, we must replace compromised historical glass with certified, high-impact polycarbonate or tempered acrylic to ensure safety under pressure. For Museum Static pieces, we carefully clean and retain the original glass.

Will structural soldering damage the original manufacturer stamps?

Our localized brazing techniques utilize precise, high-silver solder applied with strict temperature controls. This targeted approach guarantees that the surrounding copper remains stable, fully protecting all historic stamps and serial numbers from heat distortion.

Can you modify original air-control valves for modern umbilical systems?

Yes, we can fabricate custom brass adapters that allow antique helmets to interface securely with contemporary umbilical air lines, preserving the original valve housing while upgrading the input mechanism for safe, modern diving operations.

Does the restoration process destroy the authentic marine patina?

Our ultrasonic cleaning baths are calibrated to remove destructive salt and active corrosion without stripping the underlying, stabilized oxidation layer. We carefully preserve the dark, rich patina that gives historical brass its distinct character.

What guarantees accompany a hydrostatic certification?

Every Class A Deep-Submergence Refit is guaranteed against structural failure at the certified pressure depth for three years of active commercial use, provided the equipment undergoes our recommended annual inspection protocol and routine seal maintenance.

Notes from the Workshop

Our journal serves as a repository for practical knowledge, community stories, and detailed insights into our material sourcing. We document successful repair projects to demonstrate what is achievable with patience and the correct tools. We also publish comprehensive care guides designed to prevent clothing damage before it occurs, because the best repair is often the one that never needs to happen.

Repair Stories

Restoring a 1980s Work Jacket

When Thomas brought a heavily frayed, indigo-dyed work jacket into our studio, the collar had worn completely through to the underlying canvas structure. Rather than attempting to hide the damage, we documented the process of replacing the entire collar section using a contrasting heavyweight twill. The bright copper thread used for the structural reinforcement lines transformed the weak points into the jacket's most striking visual element.

Saving Moths from the Merino

Moth damage presents a specific challenge because the surrounding fibres are often structurally compromised even if they appear intact. This article breaks down the exact method required to isolate a moth hole, secure the dropping stitches, and execute a solid, tension-perfect darn. We outline the importance of using a wooden darning mushroom to maintain the garment's natural shape during the repair process.

Heritage Materials

The foundation of every durable repair lies in the quality of the materials used. We source the majority of our woven cloth directly from Pennine Loom Works, a historic regional textile mill located just twelve miles from our Hebden Bridge studio. They continue to operate shuttle looms dating back to the nineteen-fifties, producing dense, short-batch cotton and wool fabrics that possess an inherent structural integrity modern high-speed looms cannot replicate. We select specific fibre weights designed to match the garments our customers most frequently repair, ensuring that a patched knee on a pair of jeans will wear evenly over time rather than tearing away from the surrounding cloth. This commitment to regional craftsmanship guarantees that our materials perform exceptionally well under stress while directly supporting the continuation of traditional weaving methods in West Yorkshire.

Workshop Events

While our instructional guides are highly detailed, some techniques are best absorbed through direct, hands-on instruction. We host monthly mending workshops at our Hebden Bridge studio, designed to guide participants through specific textile challenges. These three-hour sessions run on the second Saturday of every month, beginning at ten in the morning and concluding at one in the afternoon. Classes are strictly limited to eight attendees to ensure our instructors can provide individual guidance. Participants are encouraged to bring one damaged garment from their own wardrobe. We supply all necessary needles, threads, and fabric offcuts required to complete the repair during the class. It provides an excellent opportunity to learn proper tension control, practice various sashiko patterns, and connect with a local community dedicated to preserving their clothing.

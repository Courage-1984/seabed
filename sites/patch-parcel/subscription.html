The Weekender Repair Parcel

A structured approach to reviving your wardrobe. Every month, we assemble a focused collection of premium mending materials designed to help you tackle specific repair projects. This service removes the friction of sourcing compatible threads and fabrics, delivering everything required directly to your door in West Yorkshire. We designed this subscription to turn the daunting task of mending into an anticipated weekend ritual.

Membership Tiers

The Apprentice Tier

Designed for those entirely new to garment restoration. For eighteen pounds per month, this tier focuses on building fundamental techniques. Each box contains foundational supplies, including versatile cotton threads, basic woven patches, standard needles, and highly detailed instructional cards covering simple repairs like sewing buttons securely and closing small, unfrayed seams. It serves as an accessible entry point into the world of visible mending.

The Journeyman Tier

Our most popular option costs twenty-six pounds per month and assumes a basic understanding of needlework. This intermediate level introduces complex challenges, such as darning large holes in heavy knitwear or executing decorative sashiko patterns across denim. The included materials reflect this step up, featuring specialised threads, distinct heritage fabrics from Pennine Loom Works, and tools designed for specific textile weights. Subscribers also receive exclusive access to our online video tutorials.

The Master Artisan Tier

Priced at thirty-eight pounds per month, this comprehensive tier provides extensive materials for large-scale, intricate restoration projects. It includes generous cuts of rare, small-batch fabrics, premium silk and wool threads, and advanced, heirloom-quality tools forged in Yorkshire. Designed for individuals who view garment repair as a serious creative discipline, this tier often features challenging assignments like reconstructing collars or replacing full jacket panels using traditional methods.

Monthly Contents & Dispatch Process

Every parcel follows a clear monthly theme. In September, we might focus on preparing winter coats, supplying heavy-duty waxed thread and dense canvas patches. Come April, the focus shifts to lighter cottons and delicate darning techniques suitable for summer shirts. The exact contents remain a surprise until delivery, but they always align practically with the shifting seasons and the types of repairs most relevant at that time of year.

Our dispatch process prioritises careful handling over automated speed. We pack all subscriptions by hand in our Hebden Bridge studio during the third week of each month. Parcels are then dispatched collectively on the twenty-fourth, ensuring they arrive on doorsteps just before the final weekend of the month. This schedule intentionally encourages setting aside quiet time on a Saturday or Sunday afternoon to spread out the materials and begin working on a garment. We use robust, recyclable cardboard packaging that fits through a standard letterbox, eliminating the frustration of missed deliveries.

Gift Subscriptions

Providing the ability to repair clothing is a practical, lasting gift that encourages sustainable habits. We offer prepaid gift subscriptions in three, six, or twelve-month durations. Rather than renewing automatically, these plans expire naturally, making them ideal for birthdays or holidays. The recipient's first box includes a handwritten welcome card explaining the service and the name of the person who gifted it to them. It is an excellent way to introduce a friend or family member to the satisfaction of visible mending without requiring them to make an ongoing financial commitment.

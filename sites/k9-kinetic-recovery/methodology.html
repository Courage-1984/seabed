Biomechanical Restoration

Validating the unconventional combination of water resistance and sound wave therapy for canine rehabilitation.

Our methodology focuses on offloading the intense mechanical stress borne by retired working dogs. Through highly specialised intervention, we mitigate the progression of osteoarthritis, hip dysplasia, and soft-tissue degradation commonly associated with high-impact service.

Underwater Treadmill Dynamics

By utilising the hydrostatic pressure and buoyancy of the aquatic environment, we reduce load-bearing weight by up to 60%. This controlled resistance environment strengthens stabilising musculature without exacerbating micro-traumas in the articular cartilage.

The warm water (maintained at 30°C) promotes vasodilation, increasing blood flow to injured tissues and accelerating cellular repair.

Acoustic Resonance Bathing

A breakthrough in palliative canine care. Low-frequency acoustic sound waves (between 30Hz and 120Hz) are directed precisely into dense connective tissues and bone.

This targeted vibroacoustic therapy stimulates osteoblastic activity and significantly reduces neuropathic pain signalling, inducing a state of profound systemic relaxation and parasympathetic dominance.

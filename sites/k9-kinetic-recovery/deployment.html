Why Book With Us

Our mobile deployment model eliminates the barriers that prevent retired working dogs from accessing the specialist rehabilitation they need.

Fully Mobile

We transport our self-contained clinical container directly to your location. By bringing the entire hydrotherapy and diagnostic suite to your door, we eliminate the severe stress of long-distance veterinary transport.

Veterinary Approved

Developed with orthopaedic specialists and delivered by RAMP-certified physiotherapists. We maintain formal referral relationships, ensuring seamless clinical communication and sharing all treatment records via a secure digital portal.

No Kennelling Required

Treatment happens without leaving the home environment. By eliminating the acute stress of kennelling, dogs maintain the parasympathetic dominance essential for effective tissue repair while sleeping in their own beds.

Priority Scheduling

Retired service dogs receive automatic priority booking in every sector. Acknowledging their extraordinary service, we ensure these complex cases are scheduled first and accommodate emergency referrals when clinically necessary.

Deployment FAQs

Treatment is available for any retired working dog with documented orthopaedic or musculoskeletal conditions. This includes military working dogs (MWDs), police K9 units, search-and-rescue dogs, and border force detection dogs. A formal veterinary referral and diagnostic imaging (radiographs minimum) are mandatory for all cases. We also accept private working dogs (e.g., farm dogs, assistance dogs) on a case-by-case basis, though priority scheduling is reserved for retired service animals.

Each session lasts approximately 90 minutes and includes a pre-treatment thermal imaging scan, the primary therapy modality (hydrotherapy, acoustic resonance, or myofascial release, as prescribed), a post-treatment cool-down and monitoring period, and a handler debrief with home exercise recommendations. The first session in any treatment block also includes a full intake assessment with validated pain scoring (CBPI/HCPI), body condition scoring, and a detailed gait analysis recorded on video for the clinical record.

We recommend submitting your handler application at least 4 weeks prior to your preferred deployment sector dates. Treatment slots are limited by the capacity of the mobile unit (typically 2–3 dogs per day depending on protocol complexity) and are allocated on a priority basis: retired military working dogs first, then police K9s, then search-and-rescue, then other working breeds. Emergency referrals for dogs in acute pain or post-surgical cases are accommodated where possible — contact us directly by telephone for urgent bookings.

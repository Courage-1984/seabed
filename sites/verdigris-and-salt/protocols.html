Treatment & Immersion Protocols

Advanced Hyperbaric Interventions. Systematic physiological restoration through highly regulated sub-aquatic treatments.

Verdigris & Salt applies rigorous scientific methodology to occupational decompression recovery. By utilising state-of-the-art pressurisation environments, we deliver targeted, measurable therapies designed explicitly for the complex needs of the commercial diving sector. Our integrated approach ensures that every variable—from precise saline concentrations to exact atmospheric pressures—is strictly controlled to facilitate rapid, profound healing for individuals returning from prolonged extreme-depth saturation assignments.

Therapy Stack

Aegir Hyperbaric Seawater Immersion (6.0 ATA)

Patients are submerged in purified, temperature-regulated seawater while the chamber is pneumatically compressed to a maximum of six atmospheres absolute. This profound pressure environment systematically compresses remaining inert gas bubbles lodged within deep connective tissues, while the hypertonic saline environment accelerates transdermal mineral absorption and cellular waste elimination.

Normobaric Halide Aerosol Flush

Following primary decompression, patients transition to targeted respiratory therapies. Operating at standard surface pressure, this protocol administers a micro-atomised, mineral-dense aerosol. The resulting halide-rich atmosphere directly treats irritated pulmonary mucosa and facilitates the rapid regeneration of essential epithelial layers damaged by prolonged synthetic mixed-gas breathing during deep saturation operations.

Sub-Ambient Thermal Cryo-Conditioning

To counter the severe systemic inflammatory responses triggered by extreme barometric shifts, this protocol employs carefully modulated cold-water immersion under controlled clinical conditions. The precise application of thermal stress forces rapid vascular constriction, heavily suppressing pro-inflammatory cytokine production and significantly reducing acute post-dive oedema across major muscular and skeletal groups.

Clinical Contraindications

To maintain our unyielding commitment to patient safety and clinical integrity, Verdigris & Salt strictly enforces absolute medical disqualifiers prior to any hyperbaric admission. We cannot proceed with compression protocols for individuals presenting with an untreated active pneumothorax, as the shifting pressure gradients pose catastrophic risks. Similarly, candidates suffering from severe pulmonary fibrosis or active viral respiratory infections are excluded due to compromised alveolar elasticity and impaired gas exchange capabilities. Unresolved tympanic membrane perforations or significant sinus blockages preclude safely achieving required therapeutic pressures. Furthermore, patients demonstrating severe claustrophobia that remains non-responsive to mild pharmacological sedation cannot be accommodated within the secure confines of the Aegir-4 chamber. Our medical directors will evaluate all pre-existing conditions during the initial intake assessment to ensure absolute safety.

Protocol Technical FAQ

What are the primary chamber pressurisation rates utilized during admission?

Our baseline compression protocol advances at a highly conservative rate of one metre per minute. This gradual approach allows for meticulous tympanic membrane equalisation and minimises acute physiological stress. For critical cases involving significant arterial gas embolism, our attending physician may authorise a rapid pressurisation sequence to immediately restore critical perfusion.

How are the specific salt concentration parameters determined for immersion?

We source our primary water column directly from the North Sea, which is then subjected to rigorous triple-filtration. The final salinity is precisely adjusted to match exact hypertonic or isotonic clinical requirements, typically ranging between thirty-two and thirty-five parts per thousand, optimising transdermal mineral absorption and cellular osmotic balance.

Does Verdigris & Salt maintain established medical insurance coverage networks?

We operate primarily through direct corporate medical retainers with major offshore energy contractors and maritime salvage corporations. We coordinate closely with specialized maritime occupational health insurers. Individual coverage verification must be completed prior to transport. Our administrative team will handle all necessary clinical documentation to expedite financial clearance procedures.

What are the anticipated residential stay durations for a complete protocol?

An average restorative programme for a diver concluding a standard twenty-eight-day saturation rotation typically requires a ten-to-fourteen-day residential admission. Complex cases presenting with persistent neurological deficits or profound cutaneous maceration may necessitate an extended twenty-one-day clinical pathway to ensure comprehensive biological stabilisation and complete functional rehabilitation.

Are patients required to remain inside the facility between treatment sessions?

While the hyperbaric immersion and halide flush therapies require strict attendance in the primary clinical zone, patients are permitted limited access to our secure dockyard recovery lounges during their normobaric intervals. We require all patients to remain within the monitored parameters of the Peterhead station to track vital physiological data continuously.

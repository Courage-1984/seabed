While each model varies slightly, the specifications below represent the typical footprint and operational needs of our flagship Mori PX-88 restorations. If you have specific venue constraints, please discuss these during your initial consultation, as we can occasionally accommodate custom modifications.

Dimensions (H x W x D) 205cm x 85cm x 165cm (including stool and curtain rail)
Operating Weight Approximately 280kg (unloaded), 310kg (fully loaded with chemistry)
Power Requirements Standard UK 240V 13A socket. Maximum draw 1500W during flash cycle.
Consumables Compatibility Accepts standard 45mm width B&W reversal paper and universal processing chemistry.

Purchase Process

Acquiring a restored analogue photo booth is a significant investment in mechanical heritage. We have developed a straightforward, transparent process to ensure that the machine you select is perfectly suited to your intended venue and operational capacity. We do not sell machines online without prior conversation, as we insist on verifying that the buyer understands the maintenance requirements of chemical photography.

  1. Initial Enquiry: Contact the workshop to discuss your venue, intended usage, and preferred machine aesthetic. We will advise on the most appropriate models currently in our inventory or on the acquisition register.
  2. Inspection & Demonstration: We strongly encourage a visit to our Hull workshop to inspect the machine, understand its mechanics, and witness a live demonstration of the development cycle.
  3. Reservation & Deposit: Once a machine is selected, a 30% deposit secures the unit. If the machine is still undergoing restoration, this deposit fixes the final price and agreed completion date.
  4. Final Testing: Prior to dispatch, the machine undergoes a final 48-hour wet-test. We provide video evidence of consistent, high-quality photographic output, ensuring absolute peace of mind.
  5. Delivery & Commissioning: The final balance is cleared, and our dedicated team transports, installs, and calibrates the machine on-site, providing comprehensive training to your staff.

Delivery & Installation

Transporting three hundred kilograms of delicate optics and chemistry requires specialist handling. Archive Arcade manages all logistics in-house using our own custom-fitted vehicles and lifting equipment, ensuring the machine arrives in exactly the condition it left our testing bench. We provide nationwide coverage across the United Kingdom, from central London galleries to remote Scottish highland retreats.

Installation is never simply a case of dropping the machine at the door. Our technicians require clear, level access to the final location. We spend approximately three hours on-site levelling the cabinet, loading the initial batch of chemistry, recalibrating the optical path to account for transit vibrations, and running test strips until perfection is achieved. Finally, we provide hands-on training for your designated operators, covering daily maintenance, paper loading, and basic troubleshooting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common enquiries about our diagnostic intake process, programme eligibility, and facility logistics.

We require a recent (within 6 months) occupational medical certificate, a signed physician's clearance for hypoxic exposure, and any relevant imaging or diagnostic reports pertaining to cardiovascular, pulmonary, or neurological conditions. If you are an active military or commercial operative, your unit medical officer's referral is sufficient in lieu of a civilian physician clearance. All documentation is processed under strict medical confidentiality protocols and stored in compliance with NHS and MOD data handling standards.

The initial on-site diagnostic assessment is a comprehensive two-day process. Day one comprises a full cardiopulmonary exercise test (CPET), resting metabolic rate analysis, body composition scanning (DEXA), and baseline polysomnography setup. Day two covers the overnight sleep data review, a cognitive resilience battery under graded hypoxic conditions, and a detailed consultation with our lead clinical physiologist to discuss your personalised programme blueprint. Remote operatives can complete the preliminary data submission online, with the on-site component scheduled within 14 days of profile acceptance.

Absolute contraindications include uncontrolled hypertension (systolic > 180 mmHg), unstable angina, severe chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), active pulmonary embolism, and sickle cell disease. Relative contraindications that require additional clinical review include pregnancy, moderate asthma, recent (under 3 months) surgical history, and concurrent use of anticoagulant medications. Our clinical team conducts a thorough risk-benefit analysis for all relative contraindications and will advise on modified protocols where appropriate. Safety is the non-negotiable foundation of every programme we design.

Our standard 12-week programme requires full residential attendance at our Northern Sea campus facility. This is a clinical necessity — the precise calibration of daily chamber sessions, continuous biometric monitoring, and nutritional protocol adherence cannot be replicated in an unsupervised environment. However, we offer an Accelerated 6-Week variant for operatives with scheduling constraints, and a post-deployment Maintenance programme that includes periodic on-site recalibration visits (typically 3–5 days per quarter) combined with remote monitoring via our wearable SpO2 and HRV platform. Contact our operations concierge to discuss bespoke scheduling arrangements.
